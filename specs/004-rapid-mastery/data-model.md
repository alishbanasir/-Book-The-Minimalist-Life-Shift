## Core Entities

### 1. 20-Hour Rule Technique

**Definition**: A skill-learning system that enables rapid acquisition of basic competence (not mastery) through 4 systematic steps; makes skill learning achievable through smart, focused practice rather than years of grinding.

**Core Philosophy**:
- **Competence ≠ Mastery**: 20 hours gets you functional, not expert
- **20 Hours = Enough**: Neither too little (5h = no progress) nor too much (100h = intimidating)
- **Smart Practice > Time**: Deliberate practice beats passive repetition

**Framework Components**:
1. Skill Analysis System (Step 1: DECONSTRUCT)
2. Minimum Learning Identification (Step 2: LEARN)
3. Barrier Removal Process (Step 3: REMOVE)
4. Deliberate Practice Commitment (Step 4: PRACTICE)

---

## Framework Structure

### Step 1: DECONSTRUCT the Skill

**Purpose**: Break overwhelming skill into learnable sub-skills

**Components**:
- **Name**: DECONSTRUCT the skill
- **Metaphor**: Like a recipe—you don't cook "dinner," you prepare ingredients, sauté vegetables, season, plate (not doing everything at once)
- **Why (Simple Rationale)**: Your brain can't learn abstract concepts. It learns concrete actions. "Play guitar" is abstract. "Press C chord shape" is concrete. Deconstruction makes skills learnable.
- **How (Action)**:
  1. List everything the skill involves (watch experts, read overviews)
  2. Identify the smallest parts (sub-skills)
  3. Pick the most critical 3-5 sub-skills to start (ignore the rest for now)
- **Example**: "Learn Spanish" feels overwhelming → DECONSTRUCT into: Vocabulary (500 common words), Pronunciation (how Spanish sounds), Grammar (present tense only), Conversation (speaking practice). Start with these 4, ignore everything else.

**Key Insight**: You don't need to master every sub-skill. Pick the critical few that unlock basic competence.

---

### Step 2: LEARN the Minimum

**Purpose**: Acquire just enough knowledge to self-correct while practicing

**Components**:
- **Name**: LEARN the minimum
- **Metaphor**: Like driving directions—you don't need a map of the entire country. You need the route to your destination.
- **Why (Simple Rationale)**: Endless learning delays practice. You don't need years of theory. You need enough to recognize "this is right" vs "this is wrong" while practicing. Minimum learning gets you to practice faster.
- **How (Action)**:
  1. Find 3-5 high-quality resources (books, videos, tutorials)
  2. Spend 2-3 hours learning basics (not weeks)
  3. Focus on: "How will I know if I'm doing this correctly?"
- **Example**: For guitar, learn how chords should sound, what finger placement looks like, how to tune (not music theory, chord construction, scales). Just enough to self-correct.

**Key Insight**: LEARN doesn't mean "become expert." It means "know enough to practice correctly."

---

### Step 3: REMOVE the Barriers

**Purpose**: Eliminate obstacles preventing practice

**Components**:
- **Name**: REMOVE the barriers
- **Metaphor**: Like clearing your desk—clutter doesn't prevent work, but removing it makes starting effortless
- **Why (Simple Rationale)**: Friction kills practice. If you have to "find equipment" or "set up" every time, you won't practice. Removing barriers upfront makes practice easy. Easy practice happens consistently.
- **How (Action)**:
  1. List everything preventing you from practicing right now
  2. Fix each barrier (buy equipment, find space, schedule time)
  3. Make practice as easy as possible (guitar visible, notebook open, app installed, time blocked)
- **Example**: For Spanish: Find language partner (now), download vocabulary app (now), schedule 30-minute daily slot (now), bookmark free resources (now). No excuses left.

**Key Insight**: Remove barriers before starting 20 hours, not during. Friction is the enemy of practice.

---

### Step 4: PRACTICE Deliberately

**Purpose**: Commit to 20 focused hours of practice with immediate feedback

**Components**:
- **Name**: PRACTICE deliberately
- **Metaphor**: Like lifting weights—random exercise doesn't build muscle. Progressive overload with proper form does. Deliberate practice with feedback builds skills.
- **Why (Simple Rationale)**: Passive repetition doesn't create skill. Deliberate practice (focused attention + immediate feedback + pushing limits) creates learning. 20 hours of deliberate practice beats 100 hours of mindless repetition.
- **How (Action)**:
  1. Commit to 20 hours (track your time: 5/20, 10/20, 20/20)
  2. Practice with full attention (no TV, no distractions)
  3. Get immediate feedback (self-correct, ask others, record yourself, compare to experts)
  4. Focus on weaknesses (not what you're already good at)
- **Example**: For guitar, practice chord transitions (boring but critical), not playing easy songs repeatedly. Time yourself switching between C and G. Record yourself. Compare to tutorial. Fix mistakes. Repeat. 20 hours of this = basic competence.

**Key Insight**: Deliberate practice is hard. That's why it works. Comfort doesn't create skills.

## 20-Hour Commitment Structure

**Definition**: Total focused practice time (not calendar time) representing real, concentrated skill-building effort.

**Time Translation**:
| Intensity | Minutes/Day | Calendar Time | Best For |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|----------|
| Consistent | 30-45 min/day | 3-4 weeks | Full-time workers, busy schedules |
| Intensive | 1-2 hours/day | 2-3 weeks | Students, sabbaticals, focused learning |
| Weekend Focus | 3-4 hours/weekend | 5-7 weekends | Busy weekdays, hobby learning |

**What Counts as "Deliberate Practice"** (see research.md for full detail):
- ✅ Active practice (playing, speaking, building, doing)
- ✅ Focused learning (notes, drills, exercises with feedback)
- ✅ Skill application (using the skill in context)
- ✅ Self-correction (identifying and fixing errors)
- ❌ Passive consumption (watching tutorials without doing)
- ❌ Mindless repetition (doing without attention or feedback)

**Simple Test**: "If someone asks 'what did you improve in that hour?', can you give a concrete answer?"

## Skill Deconstruction Examples (Concrete Applications)

### Example 1: Learn Conversational Spanish (20 hours total)
- **DECONSTRUCT**: Vocabulary (500 common words), Pronunciation (Spanish sounds), Grammar (present tense), Conversation (speaking practice)
- **LEARN**: How Spanish sounds work, basic sentence patterns, self-correction (recognizing errors when speaking)
- **REMOVE**: Find language partner (online or local), download vocabulary app (Anki/Duolingo), schedule 30-45 min daily, bookmark resources
- **PRACTICE**: 20 hours of speaking (even awkward attempts), focus on most common phrases, get feedback from partner, record yourself

### Example 2: Play Basic Guitar (20 hours total)
- **DECONSTRUCT**: Chord shapes (5-7 basic chords: C, G, D, Am, Em), Strumming patterns (down-up rhythm), Chord transitions, Simple songs (3 easy songs)
- **LEARN**: How to hold guitar, finger placement for chords, what sounds "right" vs "wrong", basic rhythm
- **REMOVE**: Tune guitar (or use tuner app), find quiet practice space, learn 3 songs you love, set guitar in visible spot
- **PRACTICE**: 20 hours of chord switching, strumming, playing songs (not reading theory books)

### Example 3: Build Basic Website (20 hours total)
- **DECONSTRUCT**: HTML structure (tags, headings, paragraphs, links), CSS styling (colors, fonts, layout), Publishing (hosting)
- **LEARN**: How HTML tags work, how CSS changes appearance, how to see changes (refresh browser), how to Google errors
- **REMOVE**: Install text editor (VS Code free), find free hosting (Netlify/GitHub Pages), bookmark learning resources (MDN, W3Schools)
- **PRACTICE**: Build 3 simple websites, focus on doing (not watching tutorials endlessly), publish each one

### Example 4: Sketch Basic Portraits (20 hours total)
- **DECONSTRUCT**: Face proportions (eyes halfway down, rule of thirds), Shading (light/dark values), Line quality (confident strokes), Reference copying
- **LEARN**: Basic proportions (where features go), how to see shapes (not symbols), self-evaluation (what looks off?)
- **REMOVE**: Buy sketchbook + pencils (cheap set fine), find reference photos (free online), set daily 30-minute slot, clear desk space
- **PRACTICE**: Draw 40 faces (30 min each = 20 hours), copy references, focus on observation (not perfection), compare to originals

## Edge Cases & Boundaries

### Edge Case 1: Skill More/Less Complex Than 20 Hours
**Scenario**: Some skills achievable in 10 hours, others need 50+
**Solution**: Adjust time but keep 4-step structure
**Content Note**: Brief mention in closing ("Simpler skills? Maybe 10 hours. Complex skills? Pick a simpler version or expect more time. 20 hours is the average for basic competence.")

### Edge Case 2: "I Completed 20 Hours But I'm Not Competent"
**Scenario**: Unrealistic expectations or wrong focus
**Solution**: Clarify competence vs. mastery, deliberate vs. passive practice
**Content Note**: Action steps emphasize deliberate practice (focused attention + feedback + weaknesses)

### Edge Case 3: Limited Time (30 min/day)
**Scenario**: 20 hours feels distant at 30 min/day
**Solution**: Reframe timeline but keep commitment
**Content Note**: Time translation table shows "30-45 min/day = 3-4 weeks—that's okay, the alternative is never starting"

### Edge Case 4: Need Equipment/Teacher/Resources
**Scenario**: Barriers feel insurmountable
**Solution**: Address in Step 3 (REMOVE barriers)
**Content Note**: Examples show free/cheap solutions (free apps, borrowed equipment, online partners)

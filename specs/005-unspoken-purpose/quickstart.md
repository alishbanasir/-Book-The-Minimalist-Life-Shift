## 10-Step Writing Process

### Step 1: Draft Opening Hook (50-75 words)

**Goal**: Acknowledge disconnection, reframe purpose as already present

**Structure**:
- Sentence 1: Acknowledge feeling (completing tasks but feeling empty)
- Sentence 2-3: Reframe (purpose isn't missing—it's unspoken, already guiding you)
- Sentence 4: Promise (this chapter teaches purpose-connection technique)

**Example Pattern**:
> "You check off your to-do list—emails answered, dishes done, workout complete—but feel empty. Here's why: you haven't connected those actions to your unspoken purpose—the values already guiding your life. [Chapter teaches technique to transform routine from obligation to meaningful expression of what matters.]"

**Validation**:
- [ ] 50-75 words
- [ ] Acknowledges disconnection WITHOUT dwelling on "wasted life"
- [ ] Reframes purpose as accessible (not requiring grand discovery)

---

### Step 2: Draft SEE Section (75-85 words)

**Goal**: Teach awareness of small actions

**Components to Include**:
1. **Bold name**: "**SEE the Small Action**" (or similar)
2. **Metaphor**: Waking mid-routine (or equivalent everyday reference)
3. **Why (1-2 sentences)**: Awareness is first step; can't transform what you don't notice
4. **How (3 steps)**:
   - Pause 5-10 seconds during mundane task
   - Name what you're doing (no judgment)
   - No analysis yet—just awareness
5. **Example**: Concrete task (washing dishes: "I'm doing this right now")

**Example Pattern**:
> "**SEE the Small Action.** Like waking up mid-routine—you're on autopilot, then suddenly aware: \"Oh, I'm doing this.\" Most tasks are invisible because you're not paying attention. Seeing creates the possibility of transformation. **How**: [3-step process]. **Example**: Mid-task, pause. \"I'm washing these dishes right now.\" That's it—just see what you're doing."

**Validation**:
- [ ] 75-85 words
- [ ] Bold name present
- [ ] Metaphor uses everyday reference (not abstract)
- [ ] 3-step process clear
- [ ] Concrete example included

---

### Step 3: Draft CONNECT Section (75-85 words)

**Goal**: Teach linking tasks to values

**Components to Include**:
1. **Bold name**: "**CONNECT to Your Unspoken Purpose**"
2. **Metaphor**: Finding the thread (connection already exists)
3. **Why (1-2 sentences)**: Connection isn't forced—it's already there; you just haven't named it
4. **How (3 steps)**:
   - Ask: "How does this serve what I value?"
   - Think of unspoken purpose (family, creativity, service, growth, presence)
   - Find authentic link (don't force positivity if not real)
5. **Example**: Same task with connection articulated (dishes → creating welcoming home for family)

**Example Pattern**:
> "**CONNECT to Your Unspoken Purpose.** Like finding the thread—each task connects to something bigger (your values), you just haven't noticed. The connection isn't forced; it's already there. **How**: Ask \"How does this serve what I value?\" Think of your purpose—family, creativity, service, growth. Find the authentic link. **Example**: \"Washing dishes creates a welcoming home for my family. I value order and care. This isn't just cleaning—I'm expressing those values right now.\""

**Validation**:
- [ ] 75-85 words
- [ ] Bold name present
- [ ] Metaphor about existing connection (not creating new one)
- [ ] Question prompt included ("How does this serve what I value?")
- [ ] Example shows authentic connection (not forced)

---

### Step 4: Draft RECOGNIZE Section (75-85 words)

**Goal**: Teach legacy-building awareness

**Components to Include**:
1. **Bold name**: "**RECOGNIZE You're Building Legacy**"
2. **Metaphor**: Drops filling cup (small actions compound)
3. **Why (1-2 sentences)**: Legacy built through thousands of small choices, not big moments
4. **How (3 steps)**:
   - Acknowledge: "This is how I show up"
   - Recognize: Doing this consistently = how you're remembered
   - No pressure for perfection—just awareness
5. **Example**: Same task with legacy framing (doing this with care = creating peaceful home)

**Example Pattern**:
> "**RECOGNIZE You're Building Legacy.** Like drops filling a cup—each small action alone seems insignificant, but thousands of them become who you are. Legacy isn't built in big moments; it's accumulated through small choices aligned with values. **How**: Acknowledge \"This is how I show up.\" Recognize that doing this consistently over time = the impact you leave. **Example**: \"By doing this with care, I'm the person who creates a peaceful home. That's what my family will remember—the parent who made life feel ordered and welcoming. That's my legacy.\""

**Validation**:
- [ ] 75-85 words
- [ ] Bold name present
- [ ] Metaphor about accumulation/compounding
- [ ] Legacy reframe (small actions, not big moments)
- [ ] Example shows how consistent action = legacy

---

### Step 5: Draft Action Steps (100-150 words)

**Goal**: Immediate implementation guide

**Structure** (4 numbered steps):
1. **Identify Purpose**: "Name what you care about most (family, creativity, service, learning)—that's your unspoken purpose"
2. **See Task**: "Next mundane task, pause and name what you're doing (just awareness)"
3. **Connect**: "Ask 'How does this serve what I value?'—find authentic link"
4. **Recognize**: "Acknowledge 'This is how I'm building legacy'—small actions compound"

**Must Include**:
- Clear numbering (1, 2, 3, 4)
- Implementable within 5 minutes total
- No tools required
- Specific prompts ("Name what you care about", "Ask 'How does this serve...'")

**Validation**:
- [ ] 100-150 words
- [ ] 4 clear steps numbered
- [ ] Each step actionable within 5 minutes total
- [ ] No external tools mentioned
- [ ] Follows SEE-CONNECT-RECOGNIZE flow

---

### Step 6: Draft Closing (25-50 words)

**Goal**: Empower immediate action

**Structure**:
- Sentence 1: Reinforce purpose already present (not waiting to be discovered)
- Sentence 2: Empower living it consciously NOW (not someday)

**Example Pattern**:
> "Your unspoken purpose is already guiding you—family, creativity, service, whatever you protect. Now you can live it consciously. Start with the next small task. See it. Connect it. Recognize you're building legacy right now."

**Validation**:
- [ ] 25-50 words
- [ ] Reinforces purpose already present
- [ ] Empowers immediate action (not "someday")
- [ ] No motivational platitudes

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### ❌ Don't Do This:

1. **Over-explaining**: "The concept of unspoken purpose refers to the underlying values and desired impact that, while not formally articulated..." → **Too complex**
2. **Jargon creep**: "Discover your authentic self's calling" → **Violates FR-012**
3. **Tool mentions**: "Journal about your purpose daily" → **Violates FR-013**
4. **Forced positivity**: "Every task is meaningful!" → **Readers won't believe inauthentic connection**
5. **Vague examples**: "Connect your work to your values" → **Too abstract**
6. **Negative framing**: "You've wasted years living without purpose" → **Violates constitutional tone**
7. **Someday language**: "Once you identify your purpose, you can start living meaningfully" → **Disempowering**

### ✅ Do This Instead:

1. **Simple explanation**: "Your unspoken purpose is what you already care about—family, creativity, service."
2. **Conversational language**: "Name what you care about most—that's your purpose."
3. **Tool-free**: "No journaling required—just pause and ask 'How does this serve what I value?'"
4. **Authentic connection**: "If the link isn't real, don't force it—some tasks are just tasks."
5. **Concrete examples**: "Washing dishes creates a welcoming home for my family."
6. **Positive reframe**: "Purpose isn't missing—it's already guiding you; now name it."
7. **Immediate empowerment**: "Start with the next task. See it, connect it, recognize you're building legacy right now."

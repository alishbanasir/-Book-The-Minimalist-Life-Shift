## Step 3: Draft Core Teaching - Step 1: CATCH (25-30 min)

### Goal
Teach readers to notice and pause when negative self-talk appears (75-90 words).

### Structure

```markdown
### Step 1: CATCH the Thought

[Bold step name + metaphor - 5-10 words]

[Why this matters - 20-25 words explaining the purpose]

[How to do it - 40-50 words with specific instructions]

[Example transformation - 10-15 words showing before/recognition]
```

### Key Points to Include

**Why It Matters**: Awareness is the first step; you can't change what you don't notice

**How To Do It**:
- Notice when the critical voice appears
- Pause instead of automatically believing it
- Observe the thought as separate from yourself
- Don't judge yourself for having the thought

**Example**: "I'll never finish this project" ‚Üí Pause and recognize this is the critical voice speaking

### Writing Tips

- Use **active voice**: "You catch the thought" (not "the thought is caught")
- **Bold** the step name: "**CATCH the Thought**"
- Keep metaphor simple: catching a ball, noticing a sound
- Example should be relatable (procrastination, overwhelm)

### Self-Check

- [ ] 75-90 words for this step
- [ ] Step name in bold with "CATCH" in all caps
- [ ] Why + how + example all included
- [ ] Language at 8th-grade level
- [ ] Positive tone maintained

**Word count**: _____ / 90 max

## Step 4: Draft Core Teaching - Step 2: CHALLENGE (25-30 min)

### Goal
Teach readers to question the truth of negative thoughts (75-90 words).

### Structure

```markdown
### Step 2: CHALLENGE the Truth

[Bold step name + metaphor - 5-10 words]

[Why this matters - 20-25 words explaining the purpose]

[How to do it - 40-50 words with specific instructions]

[Example transformation - 10-15 words showing questioning process]
```

### Key Points to Include

**Why It Matters**: Thoughts are not facts; the critical voice often exaggerates or lies

**How To Do It**:
- Ask: "Is this actually true?"
- Demand evidence for the claim
- Look for counter-examples
- Recognize you're questioning the thought, not yourself

**Example**: "I'll never finish" ‚Üí Is "never" really true? Have I finished projects before?

### Writing Tips

- Use **questions**: Show readers the exact questions to ask
- **Courtroom metaphor**: Cross-examining a witness
- Keep it brief: Don't over-explain CBT theory
- Example continues from Step 1 (same negative thought)

### Self-Check

- [ ] 75-90 words for this step
- [ ] Step name in bold with "CHALLENGE" in all caps
- [ ] Why + how + example all included
- [ ] Includes 2-3 specific challenge questions
- [ ] Connects to Step 1 example

**Word count**: _____ / 90 max

## Step 5: Draft Core Teaching - Step 3: CHANGE (25-30 min)

### Goal
Teach readers to transform negative thought into immediate positive action (75-90 words).

### Structure

```markdown
### Step 3: CHANGE to Action

[Bold step name + metaphor - 5-10 words]

[Why this matters - 20-25 words explaining the purpose]

[How to do it - 40-50 words with specific instructions]

[Example transformation - 10-15 words showing final action]
```

### Key Points to Include

**Why It Matters**: Action breaks the rumination cycle; small steps create momentum

**How To Do It**:
- Identify ONE small action you can take right now
- Make it specific and time-bound
- Focus on what you CAN control
- Do it within minutes or hours (not days)

**Example**: "I'll never finish" ‚Üí "I'll work on this for 15 minutes right now"

### Writing Tips

- Emphasize **immediacy**: Minutes or hours, not someday
- Emphasize **specificity**: "Work for 15 minutes" not "try harder"
- Use **strong action verbs**: work, create, call, write, clean
- Show complete transformation: negative ‚Üí positive action

### Self-Check

- [ ] 75-90 words for this step
- [ ] Step name in bold with "CHANGE" in all caps
- [ ] Why + how + example all included
- [ ] Action is specific and immediate
- [ ] Completes the full transformation from Step 1

## Step 7: Draft Action Steps (20-25 min)

### Goal
Give readers 3-4 immediate actions to implement technique within 10 minutes (100-150 words).

### Structure

```markdown
### Your Next Steps

1. **[Action verb + object]** (25-35 words)
   [What to do + brief why]

2. **[Action verb + object]** (25-35 words)
   [What to do + brief why]

3. **[Action verb + object]** (25-35 words)
   [What to do + brief why]

(Optional 4th action if under word limit)
```

### Required Actions

**Action 1: Apply the Technique Now**
- What: "Think of one negative thought you had today. Run it through CATCH-CHALLENGE-CHANGE right now."
- Why: "Immediate practice cements the technique better than reading alone."

**Action 2: Create a Reminder**
- What: "Write the three C's (Catch, Challenge, Change) on a sticky note or phone lock screen."
- Why: "Visual reminders help you remember when negative thoughts strike."

**Action 3: Share or Plan**
- What: "Tell someone about this technique, or write when you'll use it tomorrow."
- Why: "Sharing or planning increases your commitment to actually doing it."

### Writing Tips

- **Number the list**: 1, 2, 3
- **Bold the action**: Make it stand out
- **Use "you" language**: Second person, direct
- **Keep why brief**: One sentence max per action

### Self-Check

- [ ] 100-150 words total
- [ ] 3-4 numbered actions
- [ ] Each action is specific (not vague)
- [ ] Can be completed in 10 minutes combined
- [ ] Positive, encouraging tone

**Word count**: _____ / 150 max

## Step 10: Revise for Quality (45-60 min)

### Editing Passes

**Pass 1: Cut Filler (15 min)**
- Remove qualifiers: "maybe," "perhaps," "possibly"
- Remove redundancies: "very unique" ‚Üí "unique"
- Remove unnecessary adverbs: "really," "actually"
- Tighten sentences: Aim for 12-15 words average

**Pass 2: Strengthen Verbs (15 min)**
- Replace weak verbs: "take" ‚Üí "grab," "make" ‚Üí "create"
- Use active voice: "You catch" not "Thoughts are caught"
- Cut "to be" verbs where possible: "is important" ‚Üí "matters"

**Pass 3: Improve Flow (15 min)**
- Vary sentence length: Mix short (5-8w) and medium (12-15w)
- Check paragraph rhythm: Alternate short and long
- Ensure transitions feel natural

**Pass 4: Polish Tone (15 min)**
- Read aloud‚Äîdoes it sound friendly?
- Check for unintentional negativity
- Ensure closing is empowering

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### Content Pitfalls

‚ùå **Too Much Theory**: Keep it practical; readers want techniques, not psychology lectures
‚úì **Fix**: Cut explanations to essentials; focus on how-to

‚ùå **Vague Examples**: "Be more positive" isn't actionable
‚úì **Fix**: Use specific before/after: "I'm overwhelmed" ‚Üí "I'll tackle one task for 10 minutes"

‚ùå **Word Bloat**: Exceeded 500-word limit
‚úì **Fix**: Cut entire sentences, not just words; every sentence must earn its place

### Tone Pitfalls

‚ùå **Toxic Positivity**: "Just think positive and everything will be fine!"
‚úì **Fix**: Acknowledge difficulty briefly, then reframe: "This feels hard‚Äîand you can do it."

‚ùå **Dwelling on Problems**: Three paragraphs about why negative thoughts are bad
‚úì **Fix**: One sentence acknowledgment, then move to solution

‚ùå **Judg mental Language**: "You shouldn't think negatively"
‚úì **Fix**: "When negative thoughts appear, here's what to do..."

### Style Pitfalls

‚ùå **Jargon**: "Utilize cognitive reframing techniques"
‚úì **Fix**: "Use the Voice Changer technique"

‚ùå **Long Sentences**: 30-40 word complex sentences
‚úì **Fix**: Break into 2-3 shorter sentences (10-15 words each)

‚ùå **Academic Tone**: "Research demonstrates the efficacy of..."
‚úì **Fix**: "This technique works because..."
